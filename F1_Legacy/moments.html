Iconic Moments in F1 History

Relive the most thrilling, dramatic, and unforgettable events that shaped Formula 1.

Unforgettable Chapters of Formula 1

Senna vs. Prost: Suzuka 1990

The infamous first-corner collision at the 1990 Japanese Grand Prix between Ayrton Senna and Alain Prost, effectively deciding the World Championship in Senna's favor. A moment of intense rivalry and controversy that remains debated to this day.

Schumacher's First Ferrari Title: Suzuka 2000

After years of trying, Michael Schumacher finally clinched his first Drivers' Championship with Ferrari at Suzuka, ending a 21-year title drought for the Scuderia. This moment marked the beginning of Ferrari's dominant golden era.

Hamilton's Dramatic First Title: Brazil 2008

Lewis Hamilton secured his maiden World Championship on the very last corner of the very last lap of the Brazilian Grand Prix, snatching the title from Felipe Massa in one of F1's most breathtaking finishes.

Brawn GP's Cinderella Story: 2009

Emerging from the ashes of Honda's withdrawal, Brawn GP, led by Ross Brawn, achieved an unprecedented feat by winning both the Drivers' (Jenson Button) and Constructors' Championships in their only season. A true underdog triumph.

Vettel's Dominant Fourth Title: India 2013

Sebastian Vettel secured his fourth consecutive World Championship in dominant fashion at the Indian Grand Prix, celebrating with a memorable donut display. This cemented his place as one of F1's all-time greats.

Verstappen's Controversial First Title: Abu Dhabi 2021

The highly dramatic and controversial climax to the 2021 season saw Max Verstappen overtake Lewis Hamilton on the final lap of the Abu Dhabi Grand Prix to win his first World Championship, following a contentious safety car restart.
